The Past Passive Voice

Course: Grammar for High School Students

What is the Passive Voice?

In most sentences, the subject performs an action. This is called the Active Voice.

Active: King Jayavarman VII built Angkor Thom.

(The subject, "King Jayavarman VII," did the action, "built".)

The Passive Voice is used when the subject receives the action. The focus is on the object of the active sentence.

Passive: Angkor Thom was built by King Jayavarman VII.

(The subject, "Angkor Thom," received the action, "was built".)

When Do We Use the Passive Voice?

We use the passive voice to change the focus of a sentence.

  • When the actor is unknown or unimportant.

    Passive: My motorbike was stolen last night.

    (We don't know who stole it.)

  • To emphasize the object of the action.

    Passive: The magnificent temples of Angkor were built over centuries.

    (The focus is on the temples, not the many builders.)

  • In scientific or formal writing.

    Passive: The water samples were analyzed in the lab.

    (It's more formal and objective.)

Past Passive Forms

Past Simple Passive

For completed actions in the past where the focus is on the receiver of the action.

Form: was / were + Past Participle

Example 1: The Royal Palace in Phnom Penh was built in the 1860s.

Example 2: Many ancient statues were damaged during the war.

Past Continuous Passive

For actions that were in progress at a specific time in the past.

Form: was / were + being + Past Participle

Example 1: The bridge in Kampong Cham was being constructed when I visited last year.

Example 2: Traditional silk patterns were being woven by the artisans at the market.

Past Perfect Passive

For an action that was completed before another action or time in the past.

Form: had + been + Past Participle

Example 1: By the time the tourists arrived, the best souvenirs had already been sold.

Example 2: The ancient city of Koh Ker had been abandoned for centuries before it was rediscovered.

Past Perfect Continuous Passive

Note:

This tense is very rare and often sounds awkward. It's usually better to rephrase the sentence in the active voice.

Form: had + been + being + Past Participle

Awkward Example: The restoration of the temple had been being planned for years before the funding was approved.

Better (Active): They had been planning the restoration of the temple for years...
